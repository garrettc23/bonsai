What Bonsai does

A small agent on your side of the table.

Grounded audit

Every finding cites the exact line it came from. No hallucinated charges, no vague gripes — just the rows that don't match the explanation of benefits.

Appeal letter, in your voice

A specific, signed-off-by-you letter — not a template. Cites the codes, the policy, and the math, in the tone you set in Settings.

Email negotiation

Bonsai writes to the billing office, reads the reply, and writes back — on a real domain you can verify. You're BCC'd on every message.

Voice negotiation

For the bills that need a phone call, Bonsai dials, navigates the menu, talks to a real rep, and saves the transcript to your dashboard.

How it works

Four steps. You stay in the loop the whole way.

  1. 01

    Upload your bill and EOB.

    PDFs, photos, or screenshots. The explanation of benefits is what lets Bonsai check the bill against what your insurance actually approved.

  2. 02

    Bonsai finds the errors.

    Duplicate charges, balance billing in violation of the No Surprises Act, codes that don't match the service you received. Each one cites the line.

  3. 03

    Drafts an appeal letter.

    A real, specific letter you can read, edit, and approve before anything goes out. Attached as a PDF to the negotiation thread.

  4. 04

    Negotiates by email or phone.

    Bonsai picks the channel that fits the dispute, works the case, and surfaces every offer back to you for approval. Nothing gets settled without a click from you.

FAQ

The questions everyone asks first.

What does it cost?

Nothing during preview. We're operating Bonsai on our own dime so we can learn what real users need before we charge for it.

What kinds of bills does Bonsai handle?

Today: medical bills with an explanation of benefits. The agent is general-purpose, but medical is where the savings are biggest and the audit is most rigorous, so that's where we've focused first.

Is my data safe?

Bills, transcripts, and account data live under your account only. We don't sell, share, or train external models on your data. Read the Privacy Policy for the full rundown of what we store and why.

Email or phone — who picks?

Bonsai picks the channel based on the dispute (some appeals only work in writing; some only work on a call), but you can override it in Settings. Either way, you approve the outbound message before it goes.

What if the rep refuses?

The agent escalates: another round of negotiation, a re-aimed appeal at a different department, or a hand-off back to you with everything documented so a human (you or an advocate) can take it from there.

What if Bonsai gets something wrong?

You see every finding before any letter goes out, and every email and call before it lands. If something's off, you reject the draft and Bonsai redrafts. The point is to give you leverage, not surprises.
